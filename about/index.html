Founder's Motivation

"I started SieLabs because I saw a gap — engineering education was either too theoretical or too shallow. Students graduate knowing syntax but not systems. They can write code but can't read a codebase. I wanted to build a studio that fixes that: products that are genuinely useful, and programs that teach the way real engineering works — through practice, through contribution, through building."
