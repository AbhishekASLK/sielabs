June 10, 2026

Great engineers don't just write code; they read systems. Most engineering education focuses entirely on syntax and starting from scratch. Real engineering is about dropping into a messy, millions-of-lines codebase and navigating it with confidence.

May 24, 2026

Complexity is easy to build. Simplicity requires an active, aggressive editing process. If a system is hard to explain, it's usually poorly designed. The best architectures reveal their intent immediately.

April 15, 2026

Learn to communicate with maintainers. Open source isn't just a technical exercise; it's a social protocol. Proposing a change requires context, empathy for the reviewer, and a clear demonstration of value.
